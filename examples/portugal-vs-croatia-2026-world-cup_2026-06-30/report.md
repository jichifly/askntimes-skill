# Aggregated Forecast Report: Portugal vs Croatia, 2026 World Cup

All three raw runs completed and used the same basic interpretation: exact scores are for 90 minutes plus stoppage time, not extra time or penalties. None reported using betting odds, prediction markets, polls, human-vote forecasts, or third-party simulations.

## Consensus

The runs agree on the broad match shape: Portugal are a modest 90-minute favorite, but the game is expected to be low-margin and draw-prone. The recurring reasons are Portugal's deeper attacking bench, more varied chance creation, and stronger current squad profile versus Croatia's tournament experience, midfield control, and ability to slow knockout matches.

The clearest consensus cluster is:

| 90-minute score | Run probabilities | Mean | Spread |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Portugal 1-1 Croatia | 13%, 14%, 13% | 13.3% | 1 pt |
| Portugal 1-0 Croatia | 12%, 13%, 15% | 13.3% | 3 pts |
| Portugal 2-1 Croatia | 12%, 12%, 12% | 12.0% | 0 pts |
| Portugal 2-0 Croatia | 9%, 7%, 10% | 8.7% | 3 pts |
| Portugal 0-0 Croatia | 8%, 8%, 9% | 8.3% | 1 pt |
| Croatia 1-0 Portugal | 7%, 8%, 7% | 7.3% | 1 pt |
| Croatia 2-1 Portugal | 7%, 7%, 5% | 6.3% | 2 pts |
| Portugal 2-2 Croatia | 5%, 5%, 5% | 5.0% | 0 pts |
| Portugal 3-1 Croatia | 4%, 4%, 4% | 4.0% | 0 pts |

The top exact-score cluster is therefore tightly concentrated around 1-1, 1-0 Portugal, and 2-1 Portugal. No run made a high-scoring Portugal win, a comfortable Croatia win, or a wide-open match the central case.

## 90-Minute Outcome Spread

The comparable 90-minute outcome probabilities were:

| Outcome after 90 minutes | Run 01 | Run 02 | Run 03 | Mean | Range |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Portugal win | 44% | 43% | 51% | 46.0% | 8 pts |
| Draw | 29% | 30% | 30% | 29.7% | 1 pt |
| Croatia win | 27% | 27% | 19% | 24.3% | 8 pts |

The draw estimate is the most stable part of the aggregation: all three runs put it around 30%. The main disagreement is not whether the match is close, but how much of the non-draw probability belongs to Portugal versus Croatia. Runs 01 and 02 have Croatia winning in 90 at 27%, while Run 03 is more Portugal-favorable at 51% Portugal / 19% Croatia.

## Dissent And Outliers

Run 03 is the meaningful dissenting sample. It selected Portugal 1-0 as the single most likely exact score at 15%, while Runs 01 and 02 selected 1-1 at 13-14%. It also gave Portugal a materially higher 90-minute win estimate than the other two runs.

This dissent appears assumption-driven rather than erroneous: Run 03 puts more weight on Portugal's depth, late-game attacking options, and Croatia's age/stamina risk. Runs 01 and 02 put relatively more weight on Croatia's ability to suppress tempo and keep the match in a draw or upset band.

There is also a minor notation ambiguity in Runs 01 and 03: they list both "Croatia 1-0 Portugal" and "Portugal 0-1 Croatia," which are the same score from opposite naming conventions. The aggregation treats Croatia 1-0 as a single scoreline per run rather than double-counting it.

## Assumptions Driving The Forecast

All runs assume no major late injuries or suspensions. That matters because the forecast leans on Portugal having Diogo Costa, Ruben Dias, Vitinha, Bruno Fernandes, Bernardo Silva, Nuno Mendes, and attacking depth available, and on Croatia having the Modric/Kovacic-led control structure intact.

The shared knockout-stage assumption is also central. The estimates are for 90 minutes only. A 1-1 or 0-0 result means extra time, not a final advancement result.

Recurring tactical assumptions:

- Portugal should have more ways to create chances through Bruno Fernandes, Bernardo Silva, Vitinha, wide runners, full-backs, and second-half attackers.
- Croatia can keep the score low through midfield management, set-piece quality, veteran composure, and knockout experience.
- Croatia's age and stamina are the main reason Portugal's narrow-win scores rate higher than Croatia's.
- Portugal's mixed group-stage profile keeps the runs from treating them as a heavy favorite.

## Advancement Versus Exact Score

The raw runs did not provide calibrated advancement probabilities. Where they discussed extra time or penalties, they generally still leaned Portugal slightly to advance because of depth and fresher attacking options. However, Croatia's tournament history and penalty/extra-time comfort were repeatedly cited as reasons not to equate "Portugal favored" with "Portugal safe."

The key separation is:

- Most likely 90-minute exact-score band: 1-1, Portugal 1-0, Portugal 2-1.
- Most likely 90-minute direction: Portugal modestly favored, with a very live draw.
- Advancement expectation if level after 90: Portugal slight edge, but not quantified in a comparable way by the runs.

## Bottom Line

Aggregating the three runs, the best single 90-minute exact-score estimates are effectively tied between Portugal 1-1 Croatia and Portugal 1-0 Croatia, both around 13%, with Portugal 2-1 close behind at 12%. The consensus forecast is a cautious, low-event knockout match: Portugal have the better talent and depth, Croatia have enough control and tournament craft to make extra time or a one-goal upset realistic. Confidence is moderate-low because N=3 is small and the runs share the same prompt family and factual packet, creating correlation risk.
